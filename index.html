About Water Quality

Water quality refers to the chemical, physical, and biological characteristics of water, usually with respect to its suitability for a particular purpose — most commonly drinking, irrigation, or industrial use.

Globally, 2 billion people lack access to safe drinking water. Contaminated water spreads diseases like cholera, typhoid, and dysentery, responsible for hundreds of thousands of deaths annually. Monitoring water quality using data and AI helps communities identify risks early and take preventive action.

Key parameters used to assess water quality include pH (acidity/alkalinity), Turbidity (cloudiness), and Total Dissolved Solids (TDS) — each revealing a different aspect of water health.

Parameters Explained

⚗️

pH Value

pH measures how acidic or alkaline water is on a scale of 0 to 14. A pH of 7 is neutral. Values below 7 are acidic; above 7 are alkaline. Extreme pH levels can corrode pipes and make water taste metallic or bitter.

✔ Safe: 6.5 – 8.5
🧂

Total Dissolved Solids (TDS)

TDS measures the total concentration of dissolved substances — minerals, salts, and metals — in water. Low TDS water tastes flat; very high TDS indicates contamination with harmful minerals.

✔ Safe: < 300 mg/L
🌊

Turbidity

Turbidity measures how cloudy or murky water is, caused by suspended particles like silt, bacteria, algae, or waste. High turbidity blocks sunlight and indicates possible biological contamination.

✔ Safe: < 5 NTU
